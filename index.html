🎯 Current Status

PhD candidate researching Kuroshio Current dynamics and oceanic-atmospheric interactions using satellite remote sensing and reanalysis data

Hi, I'm a highly motivated marine environmental scientist with advanced training in physical oceanography, remote sensing, and quantitative analysis of oceanic and atmospheric processes.

My research focuses on understanding mesoscale to large-scale ocean current dynamics, particularly the Kuroshio Current system and its impact on regional oceanography. I specialize in satellite data interpretation, conducting advanced analyses of sea surface temperature, chlorophyll-a concentrations, precipitation patterns, and geostrophic currents. My work combines observational data from autonomous ocean robotics with remotely sensed satellite data and reanalysis data to advance our understanding of air-sea interactions and their role in climate dynamics.
